The Locked Lead model

Every other CRO shares
your lead across twelve trials.

Big CROs survive on utilization: senior people billed across many accounts simultaneously. When your trial is one of twelve on a lead's plate, attention drifts. Timelines slip. You feel it in slow answers, rotating contacts, and decisions that sit open for weeks. Kindrial is structured to make that impossible.

What actually goes wrong at big CROs

It is rarely fraud. It is attention. Senior people rotate. Junior people fill the gaps. You were sold an "A team" at bid; you have a "C team" by Month 4. These are not Kindrial opinions — they are documented sponsor complaints in industry surveys, published in Applied Clinical Trials and Clinical Leader.

→The project manager you liked at kickoff is moved to a larger account. A replacement is assigned without your input.
→The medical monitor is shared across four studies. Your AE narrative waits ten days for a first draft.
→Sponsor decisions sit open because nobody with authority is checking the queue daily.
→You escalate. You are told the team is "at capacity." You have no remedy in the contract.

Short Mutual Confidential Disclosure Agreement — Intake Version

  1. Parties. The disclosing party and Kindrial may exchange confidential information to evaluate a possible clinical research services engagement.
  2. Purpose. Confidential information may be used only to assess study fit, proposal scope, budget assumptions, vendor feasibility, and possible contracting.
  3. Confidential information. Includes non-public protocol, product, clinical, regulatory, manufacturing, commercial, financial, vendor, and operational information disclosed in writing, orally, visually, or by upload.
  4. Obligations. The receiving party will use reasonable care, restrict access to personnel and consultants with a need to know, and not disclose or use the information outside the purpose.
  5. Exclusions. Confidential information does not include information already known without restriction, publicly available through no fault of the receiver, independently developed, or lawfully received from a third party.
  6. Compelled disclosure. If disclosure is required by law, the receiving party will provide prompt notice where legally permitted and disclose only the required portion.
  7. Term. The confidentiality obligations continue for five years from disclosure. Trade secrets remain protected as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.
  8. No license or obligation. No IP license, service obligation, partnership, or exclusive relationship is created unless a separate written agreement is signed.
  9. Return or deletion. Upon written request, confidential materials will be returned or deleted, except one archival copy may be retained for legal/compliance purposes.
  10. E-signature. Electronic acceptance may be used as evidence of agreement by the authorized representative.
