Kindrial maintains a founder-led core and a vetted network of senior consultants and vendor partners. The named study team for your protocol is confirmed during proposal advancement and SOW execution — based on indication, study design, complexity, and sponsor needs. This is the standard model for virtual CROs and specialty consulting groups: it keeps overhead off your invoice while giving you access to the right specialists for the work that actually needs to be done.

Medical Monitoring

Board-certified physicians engaged based on indication, safety profile, and protocol complexity. Eligibility review, AE/SAE review, safety narratives, DSMB preparation, and investigator communication.

Clinical Operations

Senior project managers, CRAs, and site-facing specialists assigned based on site count, geography, enrollment model, and monitoring intensity.

Regulatory & IRB Strategy

Consultants supporting IND-facing documentation, IRB submissions, protocol amendments, safety reporting workflows, and regulatory communication planning.

Biostatistics

Independent biostatisticians supporting early-phase design, dose escalation logic, interim safety review, DSMB outputs, SAP development, and final analysis.

Data & Systems

EDC, eTMF, IRT, and reporting systems configured to fit the study — instead of forcing sponsors into expensive enterprise infrastructure.

Quality & Compliance

GCP quality support for vendor qualification, TMF oversight, audit readiness, SOP alignment, and essential document review.

Straight answers

Questions

ask us.

Is the $1.95M Phase I CRO price truly all-inclusive?+
Yes — under standard assumptions. The fixed price covers everything required to run an FDA-compliant Phase I clinical trial: the EDC license, all software required to remain FDA-compliant for a Phase I trial, two MRI scans with reading, and the full operating team needed to deliver the study. If your protocol exceeds standard assumptions (e.g., complex imaging beyond two MRIs, additional sites, specialized assays, cGMP supply), we tell you in the Study-Fit Brief — before any contract.
Why does Kindrial only run Phase I and early Phase IIa?+
Focus produces quality. Mid-to-large CROs spread their attention across Phase I-IV. We exist for emerging biotechs whose entire company depends on a clean first-in-human readout. We don't compete on global Phase III — we want to be the best Phase I partner you'll ever work with.
Do I have to sign a CDA before getting a fit assessment?+
No. You choose. Path A — describe your program in your own words, no CDA, no protocol details required. We give you an indicative fit assessment. Path B — sign a mutual CDA online (e-signature, 2 minutes) and upload your synopsis, IB, or full protocol for a complete assessment. Both paths return a Study-Fit Brief within one business day.
How does the CDA signing work online?+
Standard mutual CDA, biotech-friendly language, e-signature via DocuSign-equivalent. Two minutes from click to signed copy in your inbox. No back-and-forth with our legal team. If your legal team wants modifications, we negotiate after the Study-Fit Brief, not before.
Is the Sponsor Workspace really free?+
Yes. No per-seat license, no setup fee, no usage tier. The app is bundled into every Kindrial engagement at zero additional cost — for the entire study lifecycle. Unlimited users on your side.
What if my protocol turns out to require more than the package covers?+
We tell you in the Study-Fit Brief, before any contract. If complex imaging beyond two MRIs, additional sites, or specialized assays are needed, those are scoped transparently as add-ons with their own fixed prices. The base $1.95M never silently inflates.
What if my study doesn't fit your model at all?+
We say so — in the Study-Fit Brief. "Not a fit" is a real verdict, not a sales tactic. If your program is global Phase III, complex oncology dose-escalation, or cell & gene therapy, we'll recommend a larger CRO and probably refer you directly. Our job is to be the right answer, not the only answer.
Who actually runs the study?+
A founder-led core plus a protocol-specific team (medical monitor, regulatory lead, CRA, data manager, biostatistician). All senior. The named study team for your protocol is confirmed during proposal advancement and SOW execution. Anonymized capability profiles are provided after CDA; named CVs are provided before SOW execution.
